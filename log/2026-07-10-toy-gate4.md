## Mechanism note: the off-target groupings (whitening_diag.png)
Andreas's persistent observation decomposes into two parts:
1. REAL misplaced mass: in the model's own whitened coordinates there is a
   spurious clump near (1.1, -1.0) where truth is thin — a genuine
   conditional-sharpness error, not a rendering artifact.
2. GEOMETRIC AMPLIFICATION: the T=5 context inflates sigma_ctx to [6.5, 2.3]
   (tempered chains spread ~sqrt(T) wider), so the whitened target is
   SQUEEZED — adjacent modes sit only ~0.3-0.5 whitened units apart, right
   at the scale of the model's blur; de-whitening then stretches every
   whitened-space error by 6.5x along x1. This is a concrete mechanism for
   why (K, T=5) columns score harder than T=1 (seen in gate3e): tempered
   coverage comes at the cost of whitening resolution.
CANDIDATE FIX (logged, not implemented — for the next iteration/reconvene):
T-corrected whitening, sigma_ctx / sqrt(T), recovering the T=1 scale while
keeping tempered coverage. Would need its own pre-registered arm.
