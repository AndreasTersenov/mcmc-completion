# 2026-07-09 — M2: the in-context oracle

## Hypothesis
(P8b, 75%) In-family, the θ-posterior collapses with K = O(θ-dim) probes given
exact (E, ∇E); under family mismatch the posterior concentrates confidently on
wrong θ (no self-diagnosis) — and the true-energy SNIS certificate catches it.
(P-grad, 60%) Gradients cut the K needed for contraction by ≥2× in d ≥ 8.

## Setup
`scripts/run_m2.py` → `results/m2_{contraction,sw2,mismatch}.csv`.
Zoo v0 (stage0/m2_families.py), uniform box priors:
- gmm2 (d=2, θ=(separation, weight)), funnel2 (d=2, θ=(σᵥ, cond-scale)),
  dwell2 (d=2, θ=(a,b) barrier/well params) — dense two-stage grid posterior
  (121² primary, 241² doubling; fine grid zoomed on logpost > max−20 box).
- gmm8 (d=8, θ = one mode's mean ∈ R⁸, mirror mode at −θ, prior half-space
  breaks the ±μ symmetry) — adaptive-tempering SMC (1024 particles primary,
  2048 doubling), the P-grad case.
Contexts: 4 MALA chains × K/4 steps from overdispersed inits, K ∈ {8,32,128,512},
T ∈ {1,2,5} (tempered exploration, exact untempered (E,∇E) recorded at visited
points). R = 6 replicates (fresh θ* ~ prior + fresh chains each).
Observation model (implementation choice): Gaussian pseudo-likelihood on
context-CENTERED energies (energies are only defined up to an additive
constant across families — the certificate/model must not exploit absolute
offsets); gradients enter uncentered. This choice sets the contraction FLOOR;
the with/without-∇E comparison and the K-scaling are the pre-registered
targets, not the floor value.

**Mid-run correction (before final sweep):** the first sweep used ABSOLUTE
readout noise σ_E = σ_G = 0.05. That is broken for heavy-scale families:
funnel contexts contain neck points with energies O(10²–10³), so residuals/σ
made the likelihood a spike thinner than a grid cell and the grid argmax
aliased onto wrong θ — diagnosed via in-family funnel2 rows at K=32/128 that
"collapsed" (ratio ~2·10⁻⁴) at θ-errors ~0.4 prior-sd, with the 241² full-box
and the zoomed grid landing on DIFFERENT wrong answers (the smoking gun; both
should agree if the posterior is resolved). Fix: RELATIVE readout precision,
σ_E = 0.05·sd(E_context) + 10⁻², σ_G = 0.05·sd(∇E_context) + 10⁻² — also the
more honest model of a transformer reading energy tokens (finite relative
resolution). After the fix, full-box and zoom agree and funnel2 posteriors
are honestly WIDE in σᵥ instead of confidently wrong. Full sweep re-run; the
first sweep's numbers were discarded (never entered RESULTS.md).
Measured: (a) contraction ratio = mean_i(posterior std_i / prior std_i) vs K,
with and without ∇E; (b) sliced-W2²(posterior predictive, p*) vs K (T=1);
(c) family mismatch at K=128: context from A, zoo = B, for pairs
funnel2→gmm2, dwell2→gmm2, gmm2→funnel2 + in-family controls; report
overconfidence (contraction ratio), sliced-W2²(q, p*_true), and the
true-energy SNIS certificate (ESS/N with N-doubling 16384→32768, logẐ error).

## Expectation
- In-family: near-collapse already at K = 8 with gradients (2 unknowns, 8
  exact probes ≫ identification requirement); without gradients slower,
  visible in the K=8 column. gmm8 (8 unknowns): K=8 without gradients is
  marginal (8 equations, 8 unknowns, but probes correlated along short
  chains), with gradients K=8 gives 8×(1+8)=72 numbers → collapse. P-grad
  testable mainly at gmm8; on the 2-parameter families both arms may saturate
  at all measured K (floor set by σ_E), leaving only a floor-level gap.
- Smoke-test preview (single cases, logged before the full sweep): gmm2
  K=32 grads → ratio 0.015; gmm8 K=32 grads → 0.0037; funnel2→gmm2 mismatch →
  posterior collapsed to a single cell at the prior corner (sep→2, w→0.8),
  SNIS ESS/N read 6.5% at n=16k but fell 4.5× on doubling (divergent-weight
  signature, same false-comfort mechanism as M1's funnel rows). Expect the
  full sweep to reproduce these patterns; the "certificate catches it" claim
  is expected to hold through the N-doubling INSTABILITY, not through a
  single low ESS reading.

## Result
(final sweep, relative readout precision; medians over 6 reps, T=1)

- **In-family contraction** (posterior std / prior std; K=8→512):
  gmm2: 0.099→0.007 (E only), 0.068→0.006 (+∇E). dwell2: 0.239→0.027 vs
  0.055→0.007 — gradients break the (a,b) ridge, 4.3× tighter at K=8; E-only
  crosses the 0.1 threshold only in (128,512]. funnel2: BOTH arms plateau at
  ~0.5 until K=128, collapse at K=512 (0.006–0.009). gmm8: 0.455→0.008 vs
  0.035→0.002. Contraction-doubling gate: 479/480 pass (1 no-grad gmm8 K=8
  rep, the barely-identified regime, flagged).
- **P-grad (pre-registered, 60%): CONFIRMED at d=8, θ-dim 8 (gmm8).**
  With ∇E the 0.1-contraction threshold is met at the smallest measured K
  (8); E-only needs K ∈ (32,128] by strict medians (0.104 at K=32). So
  K_no-grad/K_grad ≥ 4, comfortably ≥ 2. At fixed K=8 the gradient arm's
  posterior is 13× tighter and its θ-error 200× smaller (0.0014 vs 0.31
  prior-sd units). Caveat: K_grad < 8 unmeasurable on the frozen K grid, so
  the ratio is a lower bound.
- **P8b in-family half: CONFIRMED, K = O(θ-dim)-ish** — gmm2/dwell2(+∇E)
  identified at K=8 with θ-dim 2; gmm8 (θ-dim 8) at K=8 with gradients,
  K≈32-128 without. NEW nuance not in the prediction: identifiability is
  PARAMETER-ANISOTROPIC under relative readout precision — funnel2's
  conditional scale c is pinned to ±0.005 at K=8 while σᵥ stays essentially
  prior-wide until K=512, because neck-point energies dominate the context's
  dynamic range and the v-marginal information sits below the 5% readout
  floor. The posterior is honestly WIDE there (not confidently wrong), so the
  contraction diagnostic correctly flags it.
- **Temperature T ∈ {1,2,5}**: no resolvable effect at R=6 (medians
  non-monotone, e.g. grid-family no-grad K=512: 0.008/0.013/0.021).
  Exact-value readout dominates; where you probe matters less than what you
  read there, in-family.
- **Sliced-W2² (posterior predictive vs p*)**: gmm2/dwell2 sit at the
  same-p* sampling floor already at K=8 (medians 0.002–0.008 vs baselines
  0.0025–0.0094) — in-family generation is solved as soon as the posterior
  localizes. funnel2 rows FAIL the N-doubling gate 21/24 (heavy-tailed
  marginals make empirical SW2 unstable); qualitatively they sit 5–20× above
  floor until K=512 then land on it, consistent with the σᵥ plateau — but
  per the backpressure rule those numbers are not reportable as quantitative.
- **Family mismatch (K=128) — P8b second half: CONFIRMED with structure.**
  Zoo posteriors are overconfident: 11/12 mismatch rows contract to ≤5% of
  prior sd around a WRONG θ, typically a prior-box corner (gmm2 zoo → sep=2,
  w=0.8, the "single blob" corner; funnel2 zoo → σᵥ=4, c=2, the "maximum
  spread" corner). The true-energy SNIS certificate:
  - catches MALIGNANT mismatch via instability: 6/12 rows fail ESS
    N-doubling (single-glance readings 0.05%–14% that move >1.6× when n
    doubles; logẐ errors up to 0.47) — same mechanism as M1's
    infinite-E[w²] rows: one reading can false-comfort, the doubling check
    does not;
  - correctly PASSES benign mismatch: dwell2→gmm2 is stable at ESS 15–26%
    with |logẐ err| ≤ 0.02 — the zoo is wrong about the family but the
    predictive covers p*, and the certificate honestly prices it;
  - controls clean: in-family ESS ≈ 1.0, |logẐ err| ≤ 0.002.

## Updated belief
The in-context identification ceiling is NOT the binding constraint: with
exact (E, ∇E) readout, tens of probes identify θ in-family even at θ-dim 8,
and gradients are worth ≥4× in probe count exactly where the project needs
them (d ≥ 8). The zoo-design lessons for the toy phase: (1) include gradient
tokens; (2) expect parameter directions whose information density in the
context is below readout precision (funnel σᵥ) — contraction diagnostics on
the θ-posterior localize them; (3) the certificate's honesty under zoo
mismatch operationally REQUIRES the N-doubling/stability check as part of the
shipped protocol, not just a point-ESS reading; (4) benign-vs-malignant
mismatch is a real, measurable distinction — the certificate does the right
thing on both, except for the mode-drop blind spot documented in M1.
