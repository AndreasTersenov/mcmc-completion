# 2026-07-09 — M1: the certificate envelope

## Hypothesis
(P8a, 85%) SNIS ESS/N decays ~exponentially in d at fixed per-dim mismatch;
usable certification at d=16 requires per-dim mismatch shrinking with d. The
deliverable is the quantitative frontier.

## Setup
`scripts/run_m1.py` → `results/m1_grid.csv`, 222 grid points, N=10⁶ each
(streaming, 12 workers, ~4 min CPU). Rows per d ∈ {2,4,8,16,32,64}:
- Gaussian target: mean shift ε/dim (9 values), proposal-variance scaling 1+ε
  (9 values, both signs)
- GMM (2 modes, separation Δ=8, equal weights): proposal mode-weight
  distortion δ ∈ {0.1..0.45}; one dropped mode
- Funnel (σᵥ=3): conditional-scale mismatch (1+ε) per dim (exact closed form —
  v-part cancels), v-scale-only mismatch (1-dim, d-independent by construction),
  moment-matched diagonal-Gaussian proposal
Per point: closed-form ESS/N where derivable, empirical ESS/N and logẐ at
N=10⁶, N-doubling stability (gate 4; ESS rel tol 0.25, logZ abs tol 0.05),
analytic 4th-moment reliability flag, logẐ bias/sd across 100 chunks at N=10⁴.
True logZ = 0 everywhere by construction.

## Expectation
Empirical matches closed forms in the reliable regime; ESS/N = exp(−D₂(p‖q))
exactly on every in-family row (D₂ = Rényi-2); mode drop invisible to the
certificate; Gaussian-vs-funnel certificate undefined (E[w²] = ∞ for ANY
Gaussian proposal against a funnel: the y|v conditional variance e^v exceeds
any fixed Gaussian tail with positive probability).

## Result
- **Closed forms confirmed**: 169/222 points are reliable+stable; there
  empirical/closed-form ESS ∈ [0.946, 1.023] and |logẐ| ≤ 0.006. The ESS(ε,d)
  surface is exp(−D₂) exactly on all in-family rows.
- **Frontier (shift)**: ε*(d) = √(ln(1/t)/d) for ESS/N target t.
  ESS/N > 1%: ε* = 1.52 (d=2), 0.76 (d=8), 0.54 (d=16), 0.27 (d=64).
  P8a's shape prediction (ε* ∝ 1/√d) is exact, not approximate.
- **Frontier (variance)**: strongly asymmetric. At d=8, 1% target: proposal
  may be up to 5.8× too WIDE (ε*=+4.78) but only ~2× too NARROW (ε*=−0.45;
  hard wall at ε=−0.5 where E[w²] diverges). Overdispersion is cheap
  insurance; underdispersion kills. Capability target for the sampler: err
  wide.
- **Certificate self-diagnosis is quartically harder than certification**
  (new, not pre-registered): for shift-type mismatch the ESS estimator's own
  relative sd is √((e^{4D₂}−1)/N), so *measuring* the certificate's health
  needs e^{4D₂} ≪ N while the certificate itself only needs e^{D₂} ≪ N. At
  N=10⁶ the measured ESS is trustworthy only where true ESS/N ≳ 4.5%
  (shift-geometry; empirically visible: d=64, ε=0.5 reads ESS 1.2·10⁻⁴ when
  truth is 1.1·10⁻⁷ — 1000× false comfort, and the N-doubling gate failed it).
  All 19 in-family ESS doubling failures lie exactly in the flagged unreliable
  regime — the gate works.
- **Mode drop (the pre-registered blind spot) — worse and more interesting
  than expected**: exact analysis (any d): w = (1+e^{−2x·μ})/2 under the
  one-mode proposal, so E[w] = 1 (Ẑ technically unbiased) but
  E[w²] = (3+e^{Δ²})/4 ⇒ true ESS/N ≈ 4e^{−Δ²} ≈ 6·10⁻²⁸ at Δ=8,
  d-independent. My initial semi-closed form (ESS≈1) was wrong; corrected and
  rows re-run (same seeds, empirical values verified unchanged). At N=10⁶ the
  measured ESS is a seed lottery: {0.87, 0.05, 0.68, 6·10⁻⁶, 0.88, 3·10⁻⁴}
  across the six d values, while logẐ locks onto −ln 2 = −0.693 with sd ~10⁻³
  (stably, confidently wrong). N-doubling caught 4/6 rows. So: the certificate
  does not see a dropped mode (confirmed, quantified: logZ biased by exactly
  the log covered mass), and the ESS diagnostic is *erratically* rather than
  reliably blind.
- **GMM weight distortion**: matches semi-closed form 1/Σ(p_j²/q_j);
  d-independent (per-mode mismatch, not per-dim); even δ=0.45 keeps ESS/N ≈ 19%.
  Mode *weights* are cheap to get wrong; mode *support* is fatal.
- **Funnel**: conditional-scale row reproduces the Gaussian scale closed form
  with d−1 (structure transfers: mismatch in conditionals prices like
  independent dims). v-scale row is d-independent as constructed. The
  Gaussian-proposal row: ESS/N reads ~10⁻⁵–10⁻⁴ at d ≥ 4 and keeps FALLING
  with N (doubling failed 4/6, logZ biased to −2.5 at d=64) — certificate
  undefined, as predicted. Caveat: at d=2 it read a stable 4.9% and PASSED
  doubling despite E[w²]=∞ — finite checks cannot fully protect against
  infinite-variance weights; tail structure must be right by construction.
- logẐ error at N=10⁴ tracks ESS as expected: sd ≈ √((1/essN−1)/N), bias
  turns on hard once ESS/N ≲ 1% (e.g. −0.39±0.61 at cf-ESS 10⁻⁷).

## Updated belief
P8a confirmed and sharpened. K-M1 does NOT fire: at d=8, ESS/N > 0.1% allows
per-dim shift up to ε=0.93 (per-dim KL ≈ 0.43) — orders of magnitude looser
than what per-target-trained neural samplers achieve on smooth targets
(literature flows report ESS/N of 10–90% at d ≈ 8–64, i.e. D₂ ≲ 2; from
memory, to be cited properly at reconvene). The real constraint M1 surfaces is
not distance but STRUCTURE: (i) never underdisperse, (ii) never drop a mode
(logZ wrong by log covered mass, invisibly), (iii) match tail/conditional
structure (no Gaussian certifies a funnel at any d), (iv) budget e^{4D₂} ≪ N
if the certificate must also self-diagnose. These become the toy-phase
capability targets.
