One shared ICSModel can serve 10 targets across the 4 train families
(d in {2,4,8}) simultaneously, evaluated with FRESH contexts (unseen chains)
— context-level generalization, the precondition for full training.

## Result, attempt 1 — GATE3-FAIL (job 15583912, 0/10; full suite stays green)
Failure taxonomy (results/gate3.json):
- gmm/dwell d=2: the mode-drop signature. The gmm-d2 eval context visits 4/6
  modes covering 46.5% of mass; the model sampled exactly those modes and the
  certificate priced it: logZ-hat = -0.78 = ln(0.46). Certificate + context
  diagnostics work exactly as stage-0 predicted; the mini-zoo model followed
  the context instead of memorizing the pool marginal. Since gate (iii)
  training pairs each context with FULL-pool samples, the optimal shared
  model memorizes full mode structure (context = routing key); 60k steps was
  too few to bind routing.
- funnels: ESS ~0.1% — hardest geometry, underfit at this budget.
- warp d=2: ESS 91%, logZ 6e-4, failed ONLY the "SW2 <= 3x same-p floor"
  bar — that criterion conflates MC sampling noise with model quality and is
  hereby AMENDED (logged, not silent): sw2 <= max(3 x floor, 0.1).
  ESS/logZ/stability criteria stay as registered — they correctly caught the
  real problems.
